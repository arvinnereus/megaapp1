Your Setup Progress

1. Initial Setup

Start Here
Change your admin login credentials

The app ships with default credentials. Change them immediately so only you can access the dashboard.

  • Open the .env file in your project folder
  • Change ADMIN_USER to your preferred username
  • Change ADMIN_PASS to a strong password
  • Restart the app (python app.py)
Set your business name

Your business name appears in the sidebar, emails, and landing pages. Set it once and it updates everywhere.

Go to Settings
Upload your headshot / brand photo

Upload a headshot photo in Settings. This face will automatically appear in your AI-generated images and videos as your brand character -- making your content personal and recognizable.

Upload Headshot

2. Connect Your API Keys

Powers AI Features
Set up AI (OpenAI or OpenRouter)

This powers Jackie AI (the chatbot) and content script generation. You need at least one:

  • OpenAI (recommended): Sign up at platform.openai.com, create an API key, add it as OPENAI_API_KEY in your .env file. Set CHAT_PROVIDER=openai
  • OpenRouter (alternative): Sign up at openrouter.ai, get an API key, add it as OPENROUTER_API_KEY. Set CHAT_PROVIDER=openrouter. This lets you use Claude, GPT-4, and other models.
Connect Stripe for payments

Stripe handles payments for your digital products store. Skip this if you're not selling products yet.

  • Sign up at stripe.com and verify your business
  • Go to Developers > API Keys in Stripe
  • Copy your Secret Key and Publishable Key into .env
  • Create a checkout link in Stripe and add it as STRIPE_CHECKOUT_URL
Set up email sending (Resend)

Resend powers email campaigns and automated follow-ups. Free tier gives you 3,000 emails/month.

  • Sign up at resend.com
  • Verify your domain (they walk you through adding DNS records)
  • Copy your API key into RESEND_API_KEY in .env
  • Set RESEND_FROM_EMAIL to your business email (e.g., hello@yourbusiness.com)

3. Set Up Your CRM

Add your first real contact

The app comes with demo contacts to show you how it works. Now add a real one -- a lead, customer, or client you're working with.

  • Go to Contacts in the sidebar
  • Click + Add Contact
  • Fill in name, email, phone, company, and set their status (Lead, Customer, Client, VIP)
  • Add a note about your relationship or last interaction
Go to Contacts
Create your first deal

Deals track your sales pipeline. Each deal moves through stages: New Lead, Contacted, Proposal, Negotiation, Won, or Lost.

  • Go to Deals in the sidebar
  • Click + New Deal
  • Link it to a contact, set the value, and choose a pipeline stage
  • As conversations progress, drag the deal to the next stage
Go to Deals

4. Create Your First Content

Generate a content piece with AI

The Content Studio generates scripts, images, videos, and captions automatically. Just give it a topic or paste a URL.

  • Go to Content > Create
  • Type a content idea (e.g., "5 tips for first-time home buyers") or paste a blog URL
  • Select the platform (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, etc.)
  • Check "Include Video" if you want a video generated
  • Click Generate and watch the pipeline work in real-time
  • When done, you'll have a script, image, captions, and optionally a video -- ready to post
Create Content Now

5. Set Up Bookings

Configure your booking calendar

Let clients and leads book time with you directly. The booking calendar is pre-loaded with Mon-Fri 9am-5pm slots.

  • Go to Bookings in the sidebar
  • Review the default availability and adjust to your schedule
  • Set slot types (consultation, follow-up, etc.)
  • Share your booking link with clients: /onboarding/book
Go to Bookings

6. Digital Products & Store

Create your first digital product

Sell ebooks, PDFs, courses, templates, or any digital download. Requires Stripe to be connected for paid products.

  • Go to Digital Products in the sidebar
  • Click + New Product
  • Add title, description, price, and delivery URL (where the file is hosted)
  • For free lead magnets, set price to $0 and type to "free"
  • Your store is live at /store -- share this link
Go to Digital Products

7. Email Marketing

Set up your first email template

Email templates are reusable messages that get sent automatically (lead magnet delivery, purchase confirmations) or manually as campaigns.

  • Go to Email in the sidebar
  • Two demo templates are pre-loaded (Lead Magnet Delivery, Purchase Confirmation)
  • Edit them with your branding, or create new templates
  • Use variables like {% raw %}{{name}}{% endraw %} and {% raw %}{{product_name}}{% endraw %} for personalization
Go to Email

8. Go Live

Final Step
Deploy to Railway and connect your domain

Railway hosts your app on the internet with a real URL. It costs about $5-10/month and takes 5 minutes to set up.

  • Sign up at railway.app (free to start)
  • Create a new project and add a PostgreSQL database plugin
  • Deploy from your GitHub repo or upload your project
  • Copy all your .env variables into Railway's environment settings
  • Railway gives you a URL like yourapp.up.railway.app
  • To use your own domain: Settings > Networking > Custom Domain, then add the CNAME to your DNS
Tip: You can run locally on SQLite for testing. When you deploy to Railway, it automatically switches to PostgreSQL. Your data starts fresh on production -- the app auto-seeds demo data on first run.

Store -- Digital product sales with Stripe.

1. Understand the Business Model

Read First
Read: How the reseller model works
You are the agency. You deploy and manage this app for clients on YOUR Railway account. The client pays you a monthly subscription. They log in and use their CRM, content tools, and storefront. They never see the code or touch the server.

Your cost per client: ~$5-10/month (Railway hosting). What you charge: $200-$500/month. That's 95%+ margin on recurring revenue.

This is the same model as Go High Level -- but you own the platform and keep 100% of the revenue. No middleman, no per-seat fees, no revenue share.

How you customize it: Pick an industry (gyms, dentists, real estate, coaches, restaurants). Customize the landing page, branding, and onboarding survey for that niche. Now you have a specialized product you can sell to every business in that category.

3. Find Your First Clients

Revenue Starts Here
Tap your personal network first

Your first clients are people you already know. Don't cold-call strangers yet -- start warm.

  • Make a list of 20 people you know who own or manage a small business -- gym owners, dentists, real estate agents, coaches, restaurant owners, contractors, salon owners
  • Send a simple message: "Hey [name], I just launched a business automation system for [industry]. It handles your website, CRM, bookings, and content -- all in one place. Can I show you a quick demo? It's pretty cool."
  • Offer a "friends and family" deal for your first 3 clients -- reduced setup fee to build your portfolio and get testimonials
  • Ask for referrals: Even if they don't need it, they know someone who does. "Do you know any business owners who need help with their online presence?"
Position yourself as a business automation expert

You're not selling "software." You're selling time savings, more clients, and less stress. Position yourself as the person who automates their business.

  • Your title: "I help small businesses automate their marketing, client management, and online presence." Put this in your social media bios, LinkedIn, business cards.
  • Your pitch website: Use your demo app's landing page as your pitch site. Customize it for the industry you're targeting. Share the URL in conversations.
  • Walk into local businesses: Bring your laptop. "I built this system for [gyms/dentists/etc]. Let me show you what it does." A live demo in person closes faster than any email.
  • Post on social media: Share screenshots of the dashboard, content pipeline, and booking calendar. "Built this for a client today. Their CRM, website, and content -- all automated."
  • Join local business groups: BNI, Chamber of Commerce, Facebook groups for local business owners. Be the "tech person" who helps businesses get online.
Learn the sales script

Follow this flow on every sales call or in-person demo:

  • Open with the problem: "Most small businesses juggle 5-10 tools for their website, CRM, email, content, and payments. That costs time and money. GHL alone is $97-$497/mo and you still have to set it up."
  • Show the solution: Demo the dashboard live. Contacts, deals, content creation, and store -- all in one place. Show the landing page, booking calendar, storefront.
  • The wow moment: Open Content > Create, type an idea, and generate a full post with script, image, and captions in under 60 seconds. Then show Jackie AI adding a contact with natural language.
  • Talk ROI: "One new client per month from better content and follow-up pays for the entire system. You're already spending this on tools that don't talk to each other."
  • Handle the comparison: "Unlike GHL or HubSpot, this is YOUR system. No per-contact fees, no usage caps, no surprise charges. Flat monthly rate."
  • Close: "Let's get your onboarding survey filled out today. I can have your system live within 48 hours."

  • Existing contact list -- CSV of their current clients/leads to import.
  • Photos & content -- Headshots, product photos, testimonials, marketing copy.

5. Deploy the Client's App

Deploy on Railway (your account, their app)

The client's app runs on YOUR Railway account. They never need a Railway login. You control the server, they pay you monthly.

  • Log into railway.app. Create a new project named after the client (e.g., "Peak Fitness Gym").
  • Add a PostgreSQL database plugin. Railway provisions it automatically.
  • Deploy the app from your GitHub repo or upload a fresh zip.
  • Railway generates a URL like client-name.up.railway.app.
Configure environment variables

In Railway dashboard > client's service > Variables. DATABASE_URL is auto-set by the PostgreSQL plugin.

# Required
ADMIN_USER=clientlogin
ADMIN_PASS=strong-unique-password
SECRET_KEY=(generate: python -c "import secrets; print(secrets.token_hex(32))")
BUSINESS_NAME=Peak Fitness Gym

# Feature toggles (disable what they didn't pay for)
FEATURE_PRODUCTS=true
FEATURE_EMAIL=true
FEATURE_BOOKINGS=true

# AI (use YOUR keys)
OPENAI_API_KEY=sk-...
CHAT_PROVIDER=openai

# Client's Stripe
STRIPE_SECRET_KEY=sk_live_...
STRIPE_PUBLISHABLE_KEY=pk_live_...

# Email
RESEND_API_KEY=re_...
RESEND_FROM_EMAIL=hello@clientdomain.com
Connect their custom domain
  • Railway > service > Settings > Networking > Custom Domain. Add their domain.
  • Railway gives you a CNAME record. Add it to the client's DNS provider.
  • SSL is automatic. Wait 5-15 min for DNS propagation.
  • Test login at app.clientdomain.com/admin
Customize branding and seed data
  • App auto-seeds demo data on first deploy so it looks active immediately
  • Update BUSINESS_NAME in env vars, swap landing page copy for their business
  • Upload their headshot in Settings for AI-generated content
  • Import their existing contacts, clean out demo data when ready
  • Configure booking calendar and onboarding survey for their industry
